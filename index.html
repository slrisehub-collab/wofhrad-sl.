Our Mission

Promoting Justice & Inclusive Governance

Founded in 2006, WOFHRAD-SL emerged in the aftermath of Sierra Leone's civil war to address widespread abuses facing women — especially in rural communities where access to justice was blocked by culture and tradition in a male-dominated society.

We strive to ensure the promotion and protection of human rights and justice, democracy, and good governance — with women leading change.

Our Impact

Changing Lives, Changing Policies

Two decades of persistent advocacy has moved the needle — from the halls of parliament to the safe spaces of rural communities.

"After the 2018 elections, only 18 parliament members were female nationwide. In 2023, 41 female mandates were directly elected to parliament."

* The Gender Equality and Women's Empowerment Act (GEWE Act, 2022) now mandates 30% female representation.

The Context

Challenges Women Face in Sierra Leone

Many women continue to suffer marginalization and discrimination in education, employment, political participation, and social justice. Issues of unequal opportunity are exacerbated by early marriage and lack of land rights.

Political Exclusion

Men often feel women should not hold leadership roles, leading to under-representation in parliament.

Literacy & Education Gaps

Low literacy and lack of quality education limit women's political and civic participation.

Gender-Based Violence

Violence during elections is used to silence and discourage women from standing as candidates.
